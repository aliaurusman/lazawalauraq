Our story

Lazawal Auraq began with a simple discomfort: too many good books read once, shelved, and forgotten — while readers a few streets away couldn't afford them new. So we buy the books Lahore has finished, grade them honestly, and pass them on. The pencil notes in the margins? A previous reader's gift.

Why buy second-hand books?

A book isn't used up by being read — it's proven. Our vision is a Lahore where a student's entire reading list costs less than one new textbook, where out-of-print Urdu classics keep circulating, and where no book's story ends on its first shelf. لازوال — because the words don't age.
